## Strategy 1: Comcast Internet — $89.99/mo

### Background & Leverage
- Long-term loyal customer with recurring payments
- Competitors offer 75% of the service for 55% of the cost
- Market rate: Verizon Fios $59.99, Spectrum $49.99, AT&T Fiber $54.99
- New customer promos widely available at $39.99–$59.99/mo

### Step-by-Step Approach
1. Call **Comcast Retention** (not general customer service)
2. Have printed competitor quotes in hand
3. Express genuine willingness to switch (have backup ready)
4. Ask for supervisor if initial agent can't help
5. Get any offer in writing before accepting

### Script (Word-for-Word)

**Opening:**
> "Hi, I've been a loyal Comcast customer for several years, but my Internet bill is now $89.99/month. I've found Verizon Fios offers 300 Mbps for $59.99 and Spectrum is at $49.99 for comparable service. Before I switch, I wanted to see what loyalty discounts or promotional rates you can offer to keep my business."

**If they say "that's our standard rate":**
> "I've been with Comcast for years and my loyalty should count for something. These competitors are offering rates nearly half of what I'm paying. Can you check for a loyalty or promotional rate? I'd really prefer to stay if we can make this work."

**If they can't match:**
> "What is the absolute best offer you can put in writing for a 12-month commitment? If it's not competitive, I'll need to switch."

**Escalation:**
> "I need to speak with a retention specialist or supervisor who has more authority. This is an important budget decision and I want to explore all options before switching providers."

### Expected Outcome
- Realistic: **$20–$30/mo savings** (new rate: $59.99–$69.99)
- Best case: **$35–$40/mo savings** (new rate: $49.99)
- Success Probability: **75%**

## Strategy 2: State Farm Car Insurance — $185/mo

### Background & Leverage
- At $185/mo ($2,220/yr), rate is above market average ($1,200–$1,600/yr)
- Annual renewal is the highest-leverage negotiating moment
- Competitors: Progressive $95–$140, Geico $85–$130, Root $80–$150
- No switching costs — policies are fully portable

### Step-by-Step Approach
1. Get written quotes from **3+ competitors** (same coverage levels)
2. Call State Farm **2 weeks before renewal date**
3. Reference specific competitor quotes by name and dollar amount
4. Ask about bundling (auto + home/renters) discounts
5. Have new policy already bound before canceling

### Script (Word-for-Word)

**Opening:**
> "Hi, my policy renews on [date]. I've received quotes from Progressive at $118/mo, Geico at $110/mo, and Allstate at $125/mo for the same coverage. I've been a customer for [X] years, but at $185/mo, your rate is significantly higher. What options do you have to bring my rate in line with competitors while keeping my business?"

**If they challenge coverage equivalency:**
> "I specifically requested identical coverage — same deductibles, same liability limits, same comprehensive/collision. These are apples-to-apples comparisons. The difference is real."

**If they say they can't reduce it:**
> "At $185/mo, State Farm is no longer a value option. I'm going to move my policy to [specific competitor] and save $700/year unless we can get closer to [target rate]. Let me speak with a supervisor about retention options."

**Closing if unsuccessful:**
> "I'll be moving forward with [competitor]. If State Farm wants to win back my business in the future, I'm open to reviewing competitive rates."

### Expected Outcome
- Realistic: **$30–$50/mo savings** (new rate: $135–$155)
- Best case: **$50–$70/mo savings** (new rate: $115–$135)
- Success Probability: **70%**
